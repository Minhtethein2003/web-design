Finland (Helsinki)

Helsinki, the capital of Finland, is known for its vibrant design scene, beautiful architecture, and proximity to nature. The city offers a mix of modern urban life and serene natural landscapes, including numerous parks and waterfront areas. Visitors can explore historic sites, enjoy Finnish cuisine, and experience the unique culture of the Nordic region. Helsinki is also famous for its saunas, lively festival scene, and friendly locals who welcome visitors to experience the charm of this northern European city. Finland `s capital is a must-visit destination for those seeking a blend of culture and natural beauty. Finland money is Euro (EUR). Finland language is Finnish and Swedish. Finland time zone is UTC +2 (EET) and UTC +3 (EEST in summer). Finland food is known for its hearty and wholesome dishes, often featuring ingredients like fish , potatoes and berries. Some popular Finnish foods include Karjalanpiirakka (Karelian pasties), Lohikeito (salmon Soup), and Ruisleipa (rye bread).

Russia (Moscow)

Moscow is the capital city of Russia and one of the world`s most historically significant and culturally rich cities, stretching across a vast area along the Moskva River and combining ancient heritage with modern development, where medieval fortifications like the Kremlin stand beside busy avenues, residential districts, universities, research centers, parks, theaters, libraries, and contemporary business districts, and where Red Square serves as a symbolic heart of the nation surrounded by landmarks such as Saint Basil`s Cathedral with its colorful domes and the State Historical Museum, while daily life moves quickly through one of the largest and most efficient metro systems in the world, famous for its ornate stations, as the city experiences long cold winters and warm summers that shape local traditions, architecture, clothing, and cuisine, and supports a population drawn from many regions and cultures, contributing to a diverse social atmosphere, with Moscow playing a central role in Russia`s political administration, economic activity, scientific progress, artistic expression, literature, music, ballet, and education, hosting major events, festivals, and international gatherings, and continuing to evolve as new technologies, infrastructure projects, and cultural initiatives reshape its skyline and neighborhoods while preserving its deep historical identity.

India(Taj Mahal)

The Taj Mahal is a world-famous white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, built in the seventeenth century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan as a symbol of his deep love and devotion to his wife Mumtaz Mahal, and it stands on the banks of the Yamuna River as a masterpiece of Mughal architecture that blends Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian design elements, featuring a perfectly symmetrical layout, a large central dome, four slender minarets, intricately carved marble walls, detailed floral patterns made with precious and semi-precious stones, elegant calligraphy from the Quran, and expansive gardens designed to represent paradise, while its appearance changes throughout the day as sunlight and moonlight reflect softly on the marble surface, creating shades of white, pink, and gold, and beyond its physical beauty the Taj Mahal represents themes of love, loss, memory, and artistic achievement, attracting millions of visitors from around the world each year who come to admire its craftsmanship, history, and peaceful atmosphere, making it not only a UNESCO World Heritage Site but also one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, and a lasting cultural symbol of India`s rich history, architectural excellence, and emotional storytelling preserved through stone for centuries.

South Korea(Gyeongbok Palace)

Gyeongbok Palace is the largest and most important royal palace of the Joseon Dynasty in South Korea, located in the heart of Seoul at the foot of Mount Bugaksan, and it was first built in 1395 by King Taejo as a symbol of royal authority, national identity, and Confucian ideals, featuring grand gates such as Gwanghwamun, wide courtyards, elegant wooden halls, stone platforms, and beautiful traditional architecture that emphasizes harmony with nature, balance, and simplicity, while structures like Geunjeongjeon served as the main throne hall for state affairs and official ceremonies, and Gyeonghoeru Pavilion stood beside a peaceful pond where royal banquets and diplomatic events were held, as the palace complex was carefully designed according to geomantic principles to reflect order and stability, though it suffered destruction during the Japanese invasions in the sixteenth century and later periods of occupation, it has been extensively restored to preserve Korea`s cultural heritage, and today it houses important museums, hosts traditional guard-changing ceremonies, and allows visitors to experience Korean history, royal culture, traditional clothing, art, and architecture, making Gyeongbok Palace a powerful symbol of resilience, historical pride, and the enduring legacy of Korea`s royal past within a modern city.

Thaiwan(Sun Moon Lake)

Sun Moon Lake is one of the most beautiful and famous natural attractions in Taiwan, located in Nantou County at the center of the island, and it is known for its calm, crystal-clear waters surrounded by green mountains that create a peaceful and romantic atmosphere, with the eastern side of the lake shaped like the sun and the western side shaped like a crescent moon, which gives the lake its unique name, while visitors can enjoy boat rides, cycling along the scenic lakeside path, walking trails, sunrise and sunset views, and seasonal mist that gently floats over the water, and the area is rich in culture and history, home to the indigenous Thao people, traditional villages, temples such as Wenwu Temple, and cultural sites that reflect local traditions, as well as tea plantations producing famous Assam black tea, making Sun Moon Lake not only a place of natural beauty but also a destination that offers relaxation, cultural discovery, and a deep connection to Taiwan`s landscape and heritage.
