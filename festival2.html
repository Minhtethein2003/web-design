Lunar New Years

Lunar New Year, also known as chinese New Year or Spring Festival, is a significant traditional festival celebrated in many East Asian countries, including China, Vietnam, Korea, and Mongolia. The festival marks the beginning of the lunar calender year and usually falls betweeen late january and mid-febuary based on the Lunar calender. The celebrated typically lasts for 15days, with various customs and traditional activities observed during this period. Common traditions include family reunions, feasting on special foods, giving red envelopes containing money for good luck, and various cultural performances such as dragon and lion dances. Lunar New Year is a time for families to come together, honor ancestors, and wish for prosperity and good fortune in the coming year.
